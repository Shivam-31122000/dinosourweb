Tyrannosaurus Rex

Late Cretaceous Period (68-66 million years ago)

The Tyrannosaurus Rex, often called T-Rex, is one of the most well-represented large theropods. It was a bipedal carnivore with a massive skull balanced by a long, heavy tail.

Triceratops

Late Cretaceous Period (68-66 million years ago)

Triceratops is one of the most recognizable dinosaurs with its large bony frill and three horns on its face. It was a herbivore that could grow up to 9 meters in length.

Stegosaurus

Late Jurassic Period (155-150 million years ago)

Stegosaurus is known for the distinctive kite-shaped plates on its back and the spikes on its tail. This herbivorous dinosaur could reach up to 9 meters in length.

Velociraptor

Late Cretaceous Period (75-71 million years ago)

Contrary to popular depictions, Velociraptors were much smaller than shown in movies, about the size of a turkey. They were feathered predators with a large sickle-shaped claw on each hindfoot.

Brachiosaurus

Late Jurassic Period (154-153 million years ago)

Brachiosaurus was one of the tallest and largest dinosaurs, with its front legs longer than its hind legs, giving it a giraffe-like stance. This massive herbivore could reach up to 26 meters in length.
