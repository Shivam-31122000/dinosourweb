Triassic Food Chain

Plants & Ferns

Herbivores

Predators

The Triassic food chain was relatively simple compared to later periods. Early conifers, ferns, and cycads formed the base, consumed by herbivores like Plateosaurus. These herbivores were hunted by early theropods and large pseudosuchians like Postosuchus.

Jurassic Food Chain

Conifers & Cycads

Sauropods

Large Theropods

The Jurassic saw the rise of massive sauropods that formed the middle of complex food webs. These giants fed on tall conifers and cycads, while smaller herbivores like Stegosaurus consumed lower vegetation. Allosaurus and other large theropods hunted both juvenile sauropods and smaller herbivores.

Apex Predators

The Cretaceous food web was the most complex of the Mesozoic. The emergence of flowering plants created new food sources. Herbivores evolved sophisticated defenses like horns (Triceratops) and armor (Ankylosaurus). Predators like T. rex developed specialized hunting techniques to overcome these defenses.

The End of an Era

The K-Pg Extinction Event

Approximately 66 million years ago, a massive asteroid struck Earth near present-day Mexico, creating the Chicxulub crater. This impact triggered catastrophic events including:

  • Global wildfires and tsunamis
  • Massive dust clouds blocking sunlight
  • Acid rain and global cooling
  • Collapse of food chains worldwide

Aftermath & Legacy

The extinction event wiped out approximately 75% of all species, including all non-avian dinosaurs. This catastrophic event paved the way for:

  • Rise of mammals as dominant land animals
  • Evolution of birds from surviving theropod dinosaurs
  • Diversification of flowering plants
  • Eventual emergence of humans millions of years later
